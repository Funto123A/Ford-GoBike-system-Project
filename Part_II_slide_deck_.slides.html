Investigation Overview¶

In the part 1 of this project, I carried out data wrangling and used Python visualization libraries to explore the dataset, starting from univariate exploration to Bivariate and Multivariate Exploration.

In this part 2 of the project, I will show the interesting relationships, trends and insights discovered in the exploratory analysis.

Dataset Overview¶

The raw dataset contains 183,412 records with 16 features for Ford GoBike System in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area. The 16 features include; duration_sec, start_time, end_time, start_station_id, start_station_name, start_station_latitude, start_station_longitude, end_station_id, end_station_name, end_station_latitude, end_station_longitude, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender and bike_share_for_all_trip.

For this analysis, i used the data for riders below the average age of 67years and the main features i used include data that describes the bike riders in terms of their age, gender and their usage category(whether they are suscribers or casual users). Also data relating to the start and end of a ride, to show the period bikes are in high or low demand. The main features are; Age(gotten from the 'member_birth_year'), member_gender, user_type, duration_min(trip duration in minutes), start_time_weekday, start_time_hour.
